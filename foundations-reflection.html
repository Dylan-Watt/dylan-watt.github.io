Reflecting on human skills

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?


i have found that as i started off i was very quick to try and get everything done. i found that i needed to slow down and take my time to read and take everything in.


the second thing i learnt about myself was how i thought and more about how my brain was working when i was learning. i left that many times i struggled to understand things going back and taking a break and thinking things over i was able to come back and learn things much better.


Third Point was getting fustrated and just giving up i did find that when something got really hard i just didnt bother to try and would leave it untill the very last thing to do. i have been able to overcome myself by breaking it down and using techniques to get throught a problem


What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?


Empathy - is about bringing you closer to other people as a team.


self awareness - looking at yourself in a mirror and changing yourself to become better and understand how you work.


Values - are guiding me to what i strive for and want to work towards


What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?


i am suprised that this was even a thing in a programming course i was expecting to spend most of my time learning HTML and CSS and JS then to be thinking about my self

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?


I cant sum up the most challenging aspects as its a reflection on me but i think the hardest thing for me was taking the time to look back at myself.


Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?


Personally yes. i feel that if the human skills was cut down and shortened i would have found that i was able to pick up on other aspects of the technical side better. the time that i spent doing human skills was longer than the time i spent doing code.


Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.


I wont say its a waste of time i do think it is good to reflect on yourself and your learning skills but i do think that it should be shorter. i was spending about 2.5 hours a day learning and 3-5 hours on weekends trying to learn and keep up some of the reading for human skills lost my attention and i found myself focusing on looking at JS code or reading a bit here and there.
