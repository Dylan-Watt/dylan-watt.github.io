Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the brain’s remarkable ability to reshape itself by strengthening, weakening, or creating new neural pathways in response to learning and enabling us to acquire new skills, adapt to change, recover from damage, and continuously evolve throughout life.


It does this by:


  • Strengthening or weakening connections between neurons.
  • Growing new connections or pruning away unused ones.
  • In some regions, even making new neurons.

Two ways that this supports people are as.


Supports Mental Health


Understanding that therapy, mindfulness, and healthy habits can physically reshape the brain gives hope for overcoming depression, anxiety, or trauma. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) uses repetition of healthier thought patterns to rewire the brain.


Boosts Learning and Performance
